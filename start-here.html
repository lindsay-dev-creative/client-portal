How to use your Client Portal

I'll check the portal daily during active work phases and respond to requests within one business day. You'll get regular project updates, either in our scheduled meetings or through the Communication Hub.

If I'm going to be out or delayed, I'll let you know in advance. My goal is to keep things clear, on pace, and as stress free as possible for you.

01Latest Update

The Latest Update in the right sidebar shows the most recent change or action tied to your project. Check here first to see what's new since you last visited.

02Resources

Admin: Contracts & Agreements, Invoices & Receipts, Onboarding, and Proposals & SOWs will live here.

Website Files & Docs: Site Files, Website Copy, and Off-boarding Files and Assets will live here.

Brand Assets: This is where you can drop any brand materials (logos, colors, images, fonts, past marketing assets). You can drag and drop from your desktop, or use the + New button in Drive to upload.

Tip

Upload originals when possible. Files ending in .ai, .eps, .pdf, or .svg are preferred for logos. Everything stays in that folder for the life of the project, so nothing gets lost in email.

I'll add files as we go, so check back here first if you're looking for something specific.

03Communication Hub

This is home base for everything project-related. Instead of digging through old email threads, use this space to submit requests, ask questions, or share anything you'd like me to look at.

To add a new line item, click into the Title column on an empty row and fill in as many details as you can. I'll get a notification and respond within one business day. Keeping everything here means nothing slips through the cracks, and you'll always have a record of what's been asked and answered.

04Client To Do's

This is where I'll assign action items to you. These are things I need from you to keep the project moving, like copy, image selections, feedback, or approvals. Each to do will have a description and a due date.

When you've completed a to do, set the Status column to Review Complete. If you have questions about anything, drop a note in the Communication Hub and I'll follow up within one business day.

How to give feedback

Clear feedback moves things forward faster. When you're reviewing a deliverable, try to be as specific as possible. Instead of "I don't like this," something like "The headline feels too long, can we tighten it?" gives me exactly what I need to make the revision.

  • Consolidate all your feedback before sending it over, one round at a time.
  • Loop in anyone whose input matters before finalizing a round.
  • If you love something, say so. Knowing what's working is just as useful as knowing what isn't.

05Project Meeting Notes

This is where I'll document every meeting we have, so you don't have to take notes or worry about forgetting what was discussed.

After each call, I'll log the date, what we covered, any decisions made, and next steps. If the meeting was recorded, you'll find the link here too, so all project meeting notes stay in one searchable place.

06Project Timeline

The Project Timeline shows every phase of your project from start to launch, with dates for each one. This is your at a glance view of where we are and what's coming next. Each item has a status so you can see what's not started, in progress, or done.

Please note, timelines can flex based on feedback rounds and revisions. If a phase shifts, the timeline here will be updated to reflect the new dates.
